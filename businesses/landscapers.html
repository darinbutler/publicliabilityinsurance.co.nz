⚠️ Key Risks Facing Landscapers in NZ

Without public liability insurance, a single claim could threaten your livelihood. These are the risks most commonly faced by landscapers in New Zealand:

01Injury from lawnmowers and ride-on equipment
02Falling trees or branches damaging property
03Damage to underground services (pipes, cables)
04Injury to bystanders from flying debris
05Back and joint injuries from manual labour
06Property damage from equipment or vehicles
07Damage to client plants or fencing during work
08Chemical exposure from pesticides or fertilisers

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the cover include tree removal?
Basic public liability covers damage your equipment causes. However, tree felling is higher risk and may need specialist tree surgery insurance. Discuss with your insurer.
What if I damage a power line or underground cable?
You're liable. Public liability covers damage you cause to third-party services. Always call Before You Dig (0800 208 696) before digging to check service locations.
Am I covered if a client's child is injured by a lawnmower?
Yes. Injury to third parties (including children) caused by your equipment or work is covered. This is exactly what public liability is designed for.
Does the cover extend to pesticide or fertiliser claims?
Standard public liability covers injury or property damage. Claims for crop failure or environmental damage may not be included. Discuss chemical work with your insurer.
